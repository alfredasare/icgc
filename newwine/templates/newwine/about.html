About

With the fulfillment of the call of God to start the church, through the founder the church was led to run with the concept of New Wine:

Mark 2:22 “And no man pours new wine into old wineskins. Otherwise the wine will burst the skins and both the wine and the wineskins will be ruined: No they pour new wine into new wineskins.”

As new wine, members of the church are encouraged to move past the limits of everyday life and break forth and claim new territories; to break the status quo. Like new wine spills when an old wineskin breaks from it being poured into it, members must break the standards of the world as they let themselves go all out for the course and sacrifice to make it work.

History

First President

Mr. Joel Tackie

Run under the auspices of the ICGC Secretariat. Founded by Minister Joel Tackie, the ministry began as a prayer meeting with Miss Shirley Addo, on 3rd September, 2008 at Paa Joe prayer grounds. With the dedication and sacrifice membership grew from 2 to 13 in the succeeding weeks. This figure has been continually added on with current membership of approximately 450. The first Sunday Service came off on 1st May, 2009 at the West End Hostel, Ayeduase. The service later moved to the Nyberg hostel at Kotei before moving to the Evandy Hostel, Bomso. From there, to the Law Auditorium, to the Old Laundry, and finally to the Africa Hall Dining Hall and currently searching for a permanent venue.
